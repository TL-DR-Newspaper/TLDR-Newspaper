What does TL:DR Mean?

It means 'too long, did not read' - a phrase commonly used to let someone know they wrote a wall of text. TL:DR newspaper knows you're busy and thus our news is as short as possible. And if you want to read more we alwasy provide the sources.

It's simple: We. Have. No. Agenda. TL:DR newspaper Will give you a unbiased summary of all news we found regarding a topic.

  • No Agenda - We have no bias, talking point, spin, frame or agenda. Just a summary of the news
  • AI instead of Journalists - Our AI can read and write faster and better then any human could.
  • Source aggregation - We follow over 60.000 news sources, so you don't have to. You just get the TL:DR of what's important.
  • Source comparison - We not only list our sources, we also explain key differences between articles by different sources.

We get news from 60.000 online sources. We compare articles by these outlets to one another and cluster the ones that have similarities using AI.

From these newsclusters we generate our own articles using the latest AI technologies. Each article contains a list of sources and a comparison on differences between the sources, so you have full transparancy.
